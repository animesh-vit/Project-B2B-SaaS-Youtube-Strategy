## Post 1

**Date:** June 22, 2026

**Main topic:**  
Founder-led sales for SaaS.

**Key insights:**

- Founders need to get good at selling before scaling ARR or hiring sales talent.
- Outbound is framed as weaker in 2026; high-intent inbound calls create more leverage.
- Sales calls should diagnose why the prospect booked, not push a generic pitch.
- Generic demos lose because buyers care about their specific outcomes.
- A clear sales process can make selling feel like service instead of pressure.

**Actionable takeaway for B2B SaaS YouTube strategy:**

- Create videos that help founders improve sales calls: discovery questions, demo structure, ICP filtering, and proposal next steps.
- Use titles around urgent founder pain, such as "Founder-Led Sales Playbook" or "How to Run a SaaS Demo That Closes."
- Make content practical and process-driven, with frameworks viewers can use immediately.

## Post 2

**Date:** June 16, 2026

**Main topic:**  
Building AI SaaS around outcomes, not tools.

**Key insights:**

- Many founders are either building outdated SaaS or thin AI wrappers.
- Strong AI SaaS ideas solve messy, recurring, urgent workflows for a clear buyer.
- The product should execute work and deliver outcomes, not just give humans another tool.
- Pricing can shift from seat-based licensing to outcome-based value.
- Human experts can stay in the loop while AI models improve.

**Actionable takeaway for B2B SaaS YouTube strategy:**

- Build videos around concrete AI SaaS ideas, buyer pain, workflow breakdowns, and outcome-based positioning.
- Avoid vague AI content; focus on specific use cases and monetizable workflows.
- Use YouTube to test demand for ideas by publishing problem/solution breakdowns before building.
